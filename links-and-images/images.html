Images

This page covers common image formats, but you may also be looking for a links and useful extras.

JPGs

JPG images are good for photos. They can handle large color palettes without significantly increasing file size. These are also good for images/photos with lots of gradients. They don't allow transparent pizels, you will see all white pixels.

GIFs

GIFs are good for animations. They do provide simple color palettes, they use transparent pixels for binary options. You can't use them for opaque levels.

PNGs

PNGs are good for diagrams and icons. They can handle opacity easily and there is no color palette limitation. A PNG is bigger than a JPG file, but they are similar quality to the human eye like the JPG.

SVGs

SVGs are amazing. They are vector-based graphics that are lossless regardless of any scaling up or down. They are extremely useful for responsive design, but they can have a large influence on file size.
